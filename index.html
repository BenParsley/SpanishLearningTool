SER D.O.C.T.O.R.

Use SER for permanent or defining characteristics. Remember the DOCTOR acronym:

  • D
    Descriptions Physical appearance, personality, nationalities, and religions Ella es alta y simpática. — She is tall and friendly.
  • O
    Occupations Professions and roles a person holds Él es médico. — He is a doctor.
  • C
    Characteristics Inherent qualities or properties of an object La mesa es de madera. — The table is made of wood.
  • T
    Time Telling time, dates, and days of the week Son las tres de la tarde. — It is three in the afternoon.
  • O
    Origin Where someone or something is from; nationality Soy de Panamá. — I am from Panama.
  • R
    Relationships Family ties and personal connections Ella es mi hermana. — She is my sister.
ESTAR P.L.A.C.E.

Use ESTAR for temporary states and conditions. Remember the PLACE acronym:

  • P
    Position Physical stance or posture of a person or object El libro está abierto. — The book is open.
  • L
    Location Where someone or something physically is right now Estoy en la casa. — I am at home.
  • A
    Actions Progressive actions in progress (used with -ando / -iendo) Estoy comiendo. — I am eating.
  • C
    Conditions Temporary states or conditions that can change El agua está fría. — The water is cold.
  • E
    Emotions How someone feels at a given moment Estoy muy feliz hoy. — I am very happy today.

PARA P.E.R.F.E.C.T.

Use PARA for destination, purpose, recipients, deadlines, and point-of-view ideas. Remember PERFECT:

  • P
    Purpose Why something is done Estudio para aprender. - I study to learn.
  • E
    Employment Who someone works for Trabajo para una empresa pequena. - I work for a small company.
  • R
    Recipient Who receives an item Este regalo es para ti. - This gift is for you.
  • F
    Future Time A specific future moment La reunion es para manana. - The meeting is for tomorrow.
  • E
    Event / Destination Where someone/something is headed Salgo para la escuela. - I am leaving for school.
  • C
    Comparison / Point of View In someone's opinion Para mi, es facil. - For me, it is easy.
  • T
    Time Limit (Deadline) When something must be finished La tarea es para manana. - Homework is due tomorrow.
POR D.R.E.A.M.

Use POR for duration, reason, exchange, movement through/around, and means. Remember DREAM:

  • D
    Duration How long something happens Estudie por dos horas. - I studied for two hours.
  • R
    Reason/Cause Why something happened Lo hice por ti. - I did it because of/for you.
  • E
    Exchange Price, trade, substitutions Pague diez dolares por eso. - I paid ten dollars for that.
  • A
    Around / Through Passing through or around a place Caminamos por el parque. - We walked through the park.
  • M
    Means / Method How communication or travel happens Te llamo por telefono. - I call you by phone.
